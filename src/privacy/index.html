PRIVACY

mimi.ganba.re is an anonymous Japanese mora ear-training tool. This page describes what it collects and why.

What's stored locally on your device

The app uses your browser's localStorage to keep:

  • A randomly-generated user id (UUID), created on your first visit
  • Per-day correct / total counts, your current streak, and per-vowel skill levels
  • A rolling log of your last 2,000 answers (mora, timestamp, ms taken)
  • A queue of unsent event records (for offline use)
  • An optional nickname, if you set one

What's sent to the server

When you answer a question, the app sends to the stats worker:

  • Your random uid
  • The mora you heard, the mora you picked, and whether you re-listened
  • A timestamp, ms taken to answer, and difficulty (number of buttons shown)

The server stores these in a Cloudflare D1 database.

Is this personal data?

The uid is a random UUID minted by your browser. With no nickname attached, it carries no link to a real person, so the event records are anonymous behavioral data — not personal data under GDPR Art. 4(1). Events are therefore sent from day 1 without a consent prompt.

Nickname (opt-in)

You can set a nickname by visiting the site with ?nick=YourName in the URL. Once set, your nickname is linked to your uid and all prior and future events under that uid become identifiable. The act of setting one is treated as your consent moment. To withdraw, clear your browser data for this site or visit with ?nick= (empty) to unset it.

Cloudflare

The site itself is served from GitHub Pages; the stats worker runs on Cloudflare Workers + D1. The worker does not read or store client IP addresses. Cloudflare may keep IPs in its own edge logs for security/abuse purposes — their data as your processor, not part of what mimi.ganba.re collects.

Third-party tracking

None. No analytics, no advertising, no third-party scripts.

Deletion

To delete your local data: clear your browser data for this site. Events already submitted under your random uid remain in the database but are not linked to you unless you've set a nickname.

To request deletion of events under a specific uid (especially relevant after setting a nickname), open an issue at github.com/golddranks/ear_training/issues.
